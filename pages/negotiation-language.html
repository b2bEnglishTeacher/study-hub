02 — THEORY

The Language of Negotiation

Negotiation is not about winning or losing — it's about finding a solution that works for both sides. In English, the language of negotiation is indirect, polite, and strategic. Using the right phrases shows professionalism, confidence, and respect for the other party.

🎯

Making Proposals

Use tentative, diplomatic language to present your position. Avoid ultimatums — frame proposals as suggestions or possibilities. Conditional structures (would, could, might) soften your tone.

"What if we considered a starting salary of €45,000? I believe that reflects the value I'd bring to the role."
⚖️

Making Concessions

Show flexibility by offering trade-offs. Use "if… then" structures to link what you're giving with what you want in return. Never give something for nothing.

"I'd be happy to take on extra responsibilities, provided there's a salary review after six months."
🛡️

Pushing Back Politely

Disagree without being confrontational. Acknowledge the other side's point before presenting your counter-argument. Use phrases like "I understand, however…" or "That's a fair point, but…"

"I appreciate the offer. However, based on my research and experience, I was expecting something closer to €50,000."
🤝

Closing the Deal

Summarise what's been agreed, confirm the terms, and express enthusiasm. Make sure both sides are clear on the next steps. End on a positive, forward-looking note.

"So just to confirm — we've agreed on €47,000 with a review in six months. I'm really excited to get started."

Key Negotiation Strategies

Do your research — know your market value before negotiating salary.
Use silence strategically — don't rush to fill pauses after making a proposal.
Focus on interests, not positions — understand what the other side really needs.
Always have a BATNA (Best Alternative to a Negotiated Agreement) — know your walk-away point.
Use "we" language to create a collaborative tone: "How can we make this work?"
Never accept the first offer — it's almost always negotiable.
03 — VOCABULARY

Useful Words & Phrases

To negotiate
To discuss terms to reach an agreement
Counteroffer
A response to an initial offer with different terms
Concession
Something given up to reach agreement
BATNA
Best Alternative to a Negotiated Agreement
Win-win
A solution that benefits both sides
Leverage
Power or advantage in a negotiation
Bottom line
The minimum acceptable outcome
Trade-off
Giving up one thing to gain another
To compromise
To accept less than ideal from both sides
Terms and conditions
The rules and details of an agreement
To reach an agreement
To find a solution both sides accept
Non-negotiable
Something that cannot be changed
