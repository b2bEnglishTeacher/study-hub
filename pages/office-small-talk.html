02 — THEORY

Everyday Office Expressions

Office small talk is the social glue of the workplace. It builds relationships, eases tension, and creates a friendly environment. Mastering common idioms and casual expressions helps you fit in and feel confident.

👋

Greetings & Openers

Beyond "Hello": "How's it going?", "How was your weekend?", "Busy day?", "Good to see you!". For Monday: "Did you have a good weekend?". For Friday: "Any plans for the weekend?".

"Hey Sarah, how's it going? Busy week? — Yeah, it's been hectic! We had that big deadline on Wednesday. How about you?"
🌤️

Safe Topics & Filler Phrases

Safe topics: weather, weekends, holidays, food, commute, sports. Filler phrases: "To be honest…", "Actually…", "By the way…", "Speaking of which…", "That reminds me…".

"By the way, have you tried the new coffee place downstairs? — Not yet! Is it any good? — Actually, it's really nice. The flat white is great."
💬

Common Business Idioms

"To be on the same page" = to agree. "To touch base" = to check in briefly. "To think outside the box" = to be creative. "To get the ball rolling" = to start. "Low-hanging fruit" = easy wins. "To circle back" = to return to a topic.

"Let's touch base on the project tomorrow. I want to make sure we're on the same page before the client meeting."
👋

Exiting Conversations Gracefully

Key phrases: "Well, I'd better get back to it", "It was good catching up!", "I'll let you get on", "Anyway, I should run — talk later!". For wrapping up: "On that note…", "Right, I need to…".

"Well, it was great catching up! I'd better get back to it — I've got a call in five minutes. Have a good one!"

Office Small Talk Do's & Don'ts

Do mirror the energy — if someone's brief, keep it short; if chatty, engage.
Do remember details — "How did your daughter's recital go?" shows you listen.
Do use idioms naturally — they make you sound fluent and approachable.
Don't discuss salary, politics, or religion — these are sensitive topics.
Don't overshare personal information — keep it professional-friendly.
Don't ignore colleagues — a simple "Morning!" goes a long way.
