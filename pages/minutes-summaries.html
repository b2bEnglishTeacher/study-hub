02 — THEORY

Anatomy of Meeting Minutes

Good meeting minutes capture what was discussed, what was decided, and what needs to happen next. They serve as an official record, keep absent colleagues informed, and ensure accountability for action items.

📝

Header & Attendees

Minutes start with metadata: date, time, location (or "virtual"), attendees, absentees, and the chairperson. This provides context and accountability.

Meeting: Weekly Product Sync | Date: 12 March 2026 | Attendees: J. Silva, M. Chen, R. Patel | Absent: K. Lee
💬

Agenda & Discussion

List the agenda items and summarise each discussion point objectively. Don't transcribe word-for-word — capture the essence. Use reported speech: "Maria suggested that…", "The team discussed…".

Item 2 — Launch Timeline: The team discussed moving the launch date from April to May. Maria noted that the QA team needs two additional weeks.
✅

Decisions & Action Items

Clearly record what was decided and who is responsible for each action, with deadlines. Use strong verbs: "It was agreed that…", "ACTION: [Name] to [task] by [date]".

Decision: The launch will be postponed to 15 May. ACTION: Raj to update the project timeline by Friday 14 March.
📅

Summary & Next Meeting

End with a brief overall summary, the date/time of the next meeting, and any items to carry forward.

Summary: Three of five agenda items were resolved. Open items (budget approval, vendor selection) to be discussed next meeting. Next meeting: 19 March, 10:00 GMT.

Minutes Do's & Don'ts

Do write minutes as soon as possible after the meeting while details are fresh.
Do use a consistent template for every meeting — it makes minutes easier to follow.
Do clearly distinguish between discussion, decisions, and action items.
Don't include every word spoken — summarise, don't transcribe.
Don't add your personal opinions — minutes should be neutral and factual.
Don't leave action items without an owner and a deadline.
03 — VOCABULARY

Useful Words & Phrases

Minutes
A written record of what happened in a meeting
Agenda
A list of topics to be discussed
Action item
A task assigned to someone with a deadline
To chair (a meeting)
To lead or facilitate a meeting
Attendees
People present at a meeting
Apologies
Formal note that someone could not attend
To carry forward
To move an unresolved item to the next meeting
To second (a motion)
To formally support a proposal
AOB (Any Other Business)
Additional topics not on the agenda
To table (an item)
To postpone discussion to a later time
Quorum
The minimum number of people needed for valid decisions
To minute
To formally record something in the minutes

Writing Exercises

Practice writing meeting minutes and summaries for the following scenarios.

Meeting Minutes

Write meeting minutes

You attended a 30-minute team meeting about a product launch delay. Write the minutes including: header (date, attendees), 2 discussion points, 1 decision, and 2 action items.

Summary Email

Summarise a meeting in an email

After a client meeting, write a follow-up email summarising the key points discussed, decisions made, and next steps.

Action Items

Create an action item list

From the following notes, extract and format clear action items: 'Sarah mentioned she'd look into the vendor pricing. Tom said he'd draft the proposal. We need budget approval from Finance by end of month.'
