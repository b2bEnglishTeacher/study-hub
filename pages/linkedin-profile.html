02 — THEORY

Building Your LinkedIn Presence

LinkedIn is often the first impression a recruiter or client has of you. Your profile should tell a story — who you are, what you do, what makes you unique, and what you're looking for. Write in first person, keep it professional but personable, and use keywords relevant to your industry.

🏷️

The Headline

Your headline is the most visible text after your name. Don't just use your job title — add value by describing what you do and who you help. Include keywords recruiters search for.

Weak: "Developer at TechCorp"
Strong: "Full-Stack Developer | Building Scalable Web Apps | React, Node.js, Python"
Better: "I help startups ship products faster — Full-Stack Developer specialising in React & Node.js"
📝

The About Section

This is your personal pitch — 3 to 5 short paragraphs. Start with a hook, describe your expertise and passions, highlight key achievements, and end with a call to action. Write in first person.

"I'm a product manager passionate about turning complex problems into simple, elegant solutions. Over the past 6 years, I've launched 12 products across fintech and e-commerce, reaching over 2M users. I'm currently exploring new opportunities — let's connect!"
💼

Experience Section

Treat this like a CV, but with more personality. Use action verbs, quantify achievements, and describe impact. Include 3–5 bullet points per role, focusing on results rather than responsibilities.

"Spearheaded the migration to a microservices architecture, reducing deployment time by 60% and server costs by 30%."
🔑

Skills & Keywords

LinkedIn's algorithm matches profiles to searches using keywords. Include relevant technical and soft skills, industry terms, and certifications. Endorse others' skills — they'll often return the favour.

Skills: "Project Management, Agile, Python, Data Analysis, Stakeholder Communication, Strategic Planning"

LinkedIn Best Practices

Use a professional photo — profiles with photos get 21x more views.
Write in first person ("I lead…" not "He leads…") — it's more authentic.
Customise your profile URL (e.g., linkedin.com/in/yourname).
Ask for recommendations from colleagues, managers, or clients.
Post and engage regularly — activity boosts visibility in search results.
