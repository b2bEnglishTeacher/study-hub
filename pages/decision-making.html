02 — THEORY

The Language of Business Decisions

Every day, professionals make and communicate decisions — from strategic pivots to operational choices. The language you use when proposing, justifying, and announcing decisions shapes how they're received and implemented.

💡

Proposing Decisions

Present options clearly with pros and cons. Key phrases: "I'd like to propose that we…", "Having considered the options, I recommend…", "There are three options on the table…"

"Having considered the three options, I recommend Option B — outsourcing the QA function. It reduces costs by 25% while maintaining quality standards."
⚖️

Justifying & Explaining Rationale

Support decisions with evidence and reasoning. Key phrases: "The rationale behind this decision is…", "This is based on…", "The data suggests that…", "We weighed X against Y and concluded…"

"The rationale behind this decision is threefold: first, the market data supports earlier entry; second, our competitors are already moving; third, our team has capacity."
🤝

Building Consensus

Not all decisions are top-down. Key phrases: "What are your thoughts on…?", "Can we all agree that…?", "I'd like to hear everyone's perspective before we decide", "Are there any objections?"

"Before we finalise, I'd like to hear everyone's perspective. Sarah, what's your view on the proposed timeline? Are there any concerns we haven't addressed?"
📢

Announcing & Implementing

Communicate decisions clearly with context, impact, and next steps. Key phrases: "After careful consideration, we've decided to…", "This decision was driven by…", "The impact on your team will be…", "Next steps are…"

"After careful consideration, we've decided to close the Dublin office and consolidate operations in Lisbon. This decision was driven by cost efficiency and team proximity."

Decision Communication Do's & Don'ts

Do explain the reasoning — people accept decisions better when they understand the "why".
Do acknowledge trade-offs openly — every decision has them.
Do invite input before finalising — but be clear about who makes the final call.
Don't announce major decisions by email alone — have a conversation first.
Don't change decisions frequently without explanation — it erodes trust.
Don't present opinions as facts — distinguish between data and judgement.
03 — VOCABULARY

Useful Words & Phrases

Rationale
The reasons or logic behind a decision
Consensus
General agreement among a group
Trade-off
A compromise where you give up one thing to gain another
To weigh (options)
To carefully consider and compare alternatives
Stakeholder
A person with an interest or concern in the decision
To propose
To put forward an idea or plan for consideration
To endorse
To publicly support or approve a decision
To overrule
To reject or reverse someone else's decision
Due diligence
Thorough research and analysis before making a decision
Risk assessment
Evaluating potential risks and their impact
To greenlight
To give approval or permission to proceed
Outcome
The result or consequence of a decision

05 — PRACTICE

Writing Exercises

Practise communicating decisions professionally in the following scenarios.

Decision Proposal

Propose a strategic decision

Your company needs to choose between building a feature in-house or outsourcing it. Write a proposal presenting both options with pros and cons, and make a clear recommendation.

Justify a Decision

Explain the rationale behind a decision

Your team has decided to postpone a product launch by three months. Write a justification explaining the reasoning, the data behind it, and the trade-offs involved.

Announce a Decision

Communicate a major decision to your team

Your department is restructuring, and two teams will be merged. Write an announcement that explains the decision, its impact, and the next steps.
