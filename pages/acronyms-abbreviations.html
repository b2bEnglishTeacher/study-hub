02 — THEORY

Decoding Business Acronyms

Business English is full of acronyms and abbreviations. They save time in emails, chats, and meetings — but only if everyone understands them. This guide covers the most common ones you'll encounter daily.

📧

Email & Communication

FYI = For Your Information. ASAP = As Soon As Possible. TBD = To Be Determined/Decided. TBC = To Be Confirmed. ETA = Estimated Time of Arrival. OOO = Out Of Office. RE: = Regarding. CC/BCC = Carbon Copy / Blind Carbon Copy.

"FYI, the meeting has been moved to 3pm. Sarah is OOO until Thursday, so please CC Mark instead. The new venue is TBC."
📊

Project & Management

WIP = Work In Progress. EOD/EOW = End Of Day/Week. POC = Point Of Contact / Proof Of Concept. MVP = Minimum Viable Product. BAU = Business As Usual. QA = Quality Assurance. UAT = User Acceptance Testing.

"The dashboard redesign is WIP — we expect to deliver the MVP by EOW. Please send feedback to Tom, who is the POC for this project."
📋

Legal & Compliance

NDA = Non-Disclosure Agreement. SLA = Service Level Agreement. T&C = Terms & Conditions. IPR/IP = Intellectual Property Rights. GDPR = General Data Protection Regulation. SOW = Statement of Work. RFP = Request for Proposal.

"Before sharing the roadmap, please ensure the client has signed the NDA. The SOW and SLA should be finalised before we begin."
💼

Finance & Performance

KPI = Key Performance Indicator. ROI = Return On Investment. P&L = Profit & Loss. YTD = Year To Date. MoM = Month on Month. ARR/MRR = Annual/Monthly Recurring Revenue. COGS = Cost Of Goods Sold.

"Our MRR is €185K, up 9% MoM. YTD revenue is €1.8M against a target of €2.4M. The P&L shows a healthy gross margin of 72%."

Acronym Do's & Don'ts

Do spell out an acronym the first time you use it, then abbreviate: "We use OKRs (Objectives and Key Results)".
Do know your audience — internal teams may know acronyms that clients don't.
Do keep a personal glossary of acronyms used in your company.
Don't overuse acronyms — too many in one sentence reduces clarity.
Don't assume everyone knows what an acronym means — especially in cross-cultural teams.
Don't make up acronyms without defining them first.

Useful Words & Phrases

FYI
For Your Information — sharing info, no action needed
ASAP
As Soon As Possible — do it urgently
TBD
To Be Determined — not yet decided
WIP
Work In Progress — still being worked on
NDA
Non-Disclosure Agreement — a confidentiality contract
KPI
Key Performance Indicator — a measurable target
ROI
Return On Investment — profit relative to cost
SLA
Service Level Agreement — guaranteed service standards
EOD
End Of Day — by close of business today
OOO
Out Of Office — not available / away
MVP
Minimum Viable Product — simplest working version
P&L
Profit & Loss — a financial performance statement
