02 — THEORY

AI in the Workplace

AI and automation are transforming every industry. Understanding the key terminology helps you participate in strategic conversations, evaluate tools, and adapt to new ways of working.

🤖

AI & Machine Learning Basics

AI = systems that simulate human intelligence. Machine Learning (ML) = AI that learns from data. "Deep learning" = complex ML using neural networks. "NLP" (Natural Language Processing) = AI understanding human language.

"Our customer service chatbot uses NLP to understand queries and machine learning to improve its responses over time."
⚙️

Automation & RPA

Automation = using technology to perform tasks without human input. "RPA" (Robotic Process Automation) = software bots doing repetitive tasks. "Workflow automation" = automating business processes. "Low-code/no-code" = building automation without programming.

"We automated the invoice processing workflow using RPA, reducing manual data entry by 85% and processing time from 3 days to 4 hours."
💬

Chatbots & Generative AI

"Chatbot" = AI-powered conversational interface. "Generative AI" = AI that creates content (text, images, code). "Prompt" = instruction given to AI. "Hallucination" = when AI generates incorrect information confidently.

"The marketing team uses generative AI to draft first versions of blog posts, but every piece goes through human review to catch potential hallucinations."
📊

AI Strategy & Ethics

"AI governance" = policies for responsible AI use. "Bias" = unfair patterns in AI decisions. "Explainability" = being able to understand why AI made a decision. "Human-in-the-loop" = keeping humans involved in AI decisions.

"Our AI governance policy requires human-in-the-loop approval for all hiring decisions made with AI screening tools, to mitigate bias."

AI Communication Do's & Don'ts

Do learn the basics — you don't need to be technical to discuss AI strategically.
Do ask "what problem does this solve?" before adopting any AI tool.
Do consider ethical implications — bias, privacy, and transparency.
Don't treat AI output as always accurate — always verify.
Don't fear automation — focus on how it can enhance your role.
Don't use AI jargon without understanding it — credibility requires accuracy.

Useful Words & Phrases

Systems that simulate human intelligence to perform tasks
Machine learning
AI that improves automatically through experience and data
Algorithm
A set of rules or instructions for solving a problem
NLP
Natural Language Processing — AI understanding human language
RPA
Robotic Process Automation — bots doing repetitive tasks
Chatbot
AI-powered conversational interface for users
Generative AI
AI that creates new content such as text, images, or code
Prompt
An instruction or input given to an AI system
Hallucination
When AI generates incorrect information confidently
Bias
Unfair patterns in AI decisions based on flawed data
Automation
Using technology to perform tasks without human input
Human-in-the-loop
Keeping humans involved in AI-driven decisions
