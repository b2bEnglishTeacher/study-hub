01 — THEORY

Navigating Organisational Change

Change is constant in business — restructuring, new tools, process changes, mergers. How leaders communicate during transitions determines whether change succeeds or fails.

🔄

Types of Change

Structural change = reorganising teams/departments. Process change = new workflows. Technological change = new tools/systems. Cultural change = shifting values/behaviours. Key phrases: "We are undergoing a…", "This transition will…".

"We are undergoing a structural change, merging the sales and marketing departments into a single revenue team effective Q2."
💬

Communicating Change

Communicate early, clearly, and often. Address the "what", "why", "how", and "when". Key phrases: "The reason for this change is…", "What this means for you is…", "We understand this may raise concerns".

"The reason for this change is to improve cross-team collaboration. What this means for you is a new reporting structure, which takes effect on 1 April."
🚧

Managing Resistance

Resistance is natural. Listen to concerns, acknowledge emotions, provide support. Key phrases: "I understand this feels uncertain", "Your concerns are valid", "Let's work through this together", "What would help you feel more confident about this?".

"I understand this transition feels uncertain, and your concerns about workload are valid. Let's schedule a one-on-one to discuss how we can support you through this."
✅

Adoption & Sustainability

Ensure change sticks through training, feedback loops, and celebrating early wins. Key phrases: "The adoption rate is…", "We've seen positive results in…", "To sustain this change, we will…".

"The adoption rate for the new CRM is 78% after four weeks. To sustain this momentum, we're running weekly Q&A sessions and publishing quick-tip guides."

Change Communication Do's & Don'ts

Do explain the "why" — people accept change when they understand the reason.
Do acknowledge emotions — change is unsettling, and that's normal.
Do celebrate quick wins to build momentum and confidence.
Don't announce change without a plan — have answers ready.
Don't ignore resistance — listen, address concerns, and provide support.
Don't assume one announcement is enough — repeat the message through multiple channels.
02 — VOCABULARY

Useful Words & Phrases

Change management
The process of guiding an organisation through transitions
Transition
The period of moving from one state to another
Resistance
Opposition or reluctance to accept change
Adoption
The process of accepting and using something new
Stakeholder
A person with an interest or concern in the organisation
Restructuring
Reorganising the structure of a company or team
To roll out
To introduce or launch something new gradually
Buy-in
Agreement and support from people involved
Quick win
An early, visible success that builds momentum
Feedback loop
A system for continuously receiving and acting on input
To phase in
To introduce something step by step over time
Champion (of change)
A person who actively promotes and supports a change

04 — PRACTICE

Writing Exercises

Write professional communications for the following change management scenarios.

Change Announcement

Announce an organisational change

Your company is migrating to a new project management tool. Write an email to your team announcing the change, explaining the reason, timeline, and what it means for them.

Address Resistance

Respond to a concerned team member

A team member has expressed frustration about a restructuring that changes their reporting line. Write a response that acknowledges their concerns and offers support.

Adoption Report

Report on change adoption progress

Write a short update to leadership on the adoption progress of a recently rolled-out CRM system. Include adoption rates, challenges, and next steps.
