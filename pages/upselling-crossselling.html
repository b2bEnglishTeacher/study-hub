02 — THEORY

The Art of Suggesting More Value

Upselling (offering a higher-tier product) and cross-selling (suggesting complementary products) are essential sales skills. Done right, they add genuine value for the customer. Done wrong, they feel pushy and damage trust.

⬆️

Upselling: Offering Upgrades

Upselling means suggesting a premium version of what the customer is already buying. Focus on the added value, not just the price. Key phrases: "Have you considered the Pro version?", "For just €20 more, you'd also get…", "Many of our clients in your sector choose…".

"Have you considered our Enterprise plan? For just €50 more per month, you'd get priority support and unlimited users — which would be ideal given your team size."
➡️

Cross-selling: Complementary Products

Cross-selling means suggesting products that complement the purchase. It should feel natural and helpful. Key phrases: "This pairs well with…", "Clients who use X often find Y helpful", "Would you also benefit from…".

"Since you're implementing our CRM, you might also benefit from our email automation module — it integrates seamlessly and most clients see a 20% increase in engagement."
🎯

Timing & Reading the Client

The best time to suggest is when the client has expressed satisfaction or identified a need you can solve. Never upsell during a complaint. Watch for buying signals: "Is there a way to…?", "We've been thinking about…".

"I noticed you mentioned wanting to expand into the German market — our localisation package would be a great fit for that. Shall I send you some details?"
💎

Value Framing & Soft Language

Frame suggestions in terms of value, not cost. Use soft, non-pushy language: "You might find it useful to…", "It could be worth considering…", "Just so you're aware…", "No pressure, but…".

"Just so you're aware, we're currently offering 20% off annual plans. It could save your team around €2,400 over the year — no pressure, but I wanted to make sure you had the option."

Upselling & Cross-selling Do's & Don'ts

Do focus on value and benefits to the client, not revenue for you.
Do use social proof — "Many of our clients find…" builds trust.
Do time your suggestion well — after a positive interaction or expressed need.
Don't upsell during complaints or when the client is frustrated.
Don't be pushy — if the client says no, respect it gracefully.
Don't suggest products that don't genuinely benefit the client.
03 — VOCABULARY

Useful Words & Phrases

Upselling
Suggesting a higher-value product or upgrade
Cross-selling
Suggesting complementary products or services
Bundle
A package of products sold together at a discount
Upgrade
Moving to a higher-tier product or plan
Value proposition
A clear statement of the benefits offered
Buying signal
A verbal or behavioural cue that a client is ready to buy
Add-on
An additional feature or product added to a purchase
Premium
A higher-quality, higher-priced version
To complement
To go well together with something else
Incentive
A reward or benefit to encourage a purchase
Retention
Keeping existing customers over time
ARPU
Average Revenue Per User — average income generated per customer

05 — PRACTICE

Writing Exercises

Practice your upselling and cross-selling skills with the following scenarios.

Upsell Script

Write an upselling pitch

A client is on your Basic plan (€29/month). Write a natural upselling pitch for the Pro plan (€49/month) which includes analytics, priority support, and team collaboration.

Cross-sell Email

Write a cross-selling email

A client recently purchased your HR management software. Write an email suggesting your complementary employee training platform, focusing on value and integration.

Value Framing

Reframe a suggestion around value

Rewrite this pushy upsell to be value-focused and client-centric: 'You should upgrade to Premium. It's only €100 more and we need to hit our targets this quarter.'
