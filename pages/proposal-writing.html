01 — THEORY

Anatomy of a Business Proposal

A winning proposal clearly identifies a problem, presents a solution, and shows the value you bring. Each section must be purposeful, persuasive, and tailored to the client's needs.

🎯

Problem Statement & Context

Start by showing you understand the client's challenge. Use phrases like "We understand that…", "Your organisation is currently facing…", "The key challenge is…". This builds trust and demonstrates you've done your homework.

"We understand that your customer support team is currently handling a 40% increase in ticket volume, leading to longer response times and lower satisfaction scores."
💡

Proposed Solution

Present your approach clearly. Explain WHAT you will do, HOW, and WHY it works. Use confident language: "We propose…", "Our approach involves…", "This solution will…".

"We propose implementing an AI-powered chatbot to handle Tier 1 queries, reducing ticket volume by an estimated 30% within 90 days."
📅

Timeline & Deliverables

Break the project into clear phases with milestones. Use "Phase 1:", "Deliverable:", "Estimated completion:". This gives the client confidence that you have a structured plan.

"Phase 1 (Weeks 1–3): Discovery & Requirements | Phase 2 (Weeks 4–8): Development & Testing | Phase 3 (Weeks 9–10): Deployment & Training"
💰

Budget & Call to Action

Present costs transparently. End with a strong, clear call to action. Use "The total investment is…", "We are confident that…", "We look forward to discussing…".

"The total investment for this project is €45,000. We are confident this will deliver a positive ROI within six months. We look forward to discussing next steps at your convenience."

Proposal Writing Do's & Don'ts

Do tailor every proposal to the specific client — generic proposals rarely win.
Do focus on benefits and outcomes, not just features.
Do include social proof — case studies, testimonials, or relevant experience.
Don't use jargon the client may not understand.
Don't overwhelm with detail — keep it clear and structured.
Don't forget the call to action — tell them exactly what to do next.
02 — VOCABULARY

Useful Words & Phrases

Proposal
A formal document offering a solution to a problem
Stakeholder
A person with an interest or concern in the project
Deliverable
A tangible result or output of a project phase
ROI (Return on Investment)
The financial benefit compared to the cost
To scope
To define the boundaries of a project
Milestone
A significant point or event in a project timeline
Value proposition
A statement of the benefits you offer
To pitch
To present an idea or proposal persuasively
Buy-in
Agreement or support from key decision-makers
Competitive advantage
What makes your offer better than alternatives
Feasibility
Whether a plan is practical and achievable
To onboard
To integrate a new client or system
