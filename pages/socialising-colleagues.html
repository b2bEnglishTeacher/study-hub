02 — THEORY

Beyond the Office

Socialising with colleagues builds trust, strengthens teams, and creates a positive work culture. Knowing how to invite, accept, decline, and navigate social situations in English helps you connect naturally.

📨

Inviting & Suggesting

"Would you like to grab lunch?", "A few of us are going for drinks after work — want to join?", "We're organising a team dinner — are you free on Friday?", "How about we order in today?"

"Hey, a few of us are heading to that new Thai place for lunch. Want to join? — Sounds great! What time are you going?"
✅

Accepting & Declining

Accepting: "Sounds great!", "I'd love to!", "Count me in!", "That sounds like fun". Declining politely: "Thanks for the invite, but I can't make it this time", "I'd love to, but I already have plans", "Rain check?".

"Would you like to come to the team quiz night? — I'd love to, but I have a family dinner. Rain check? Maybe next time!"
💬

Conversations at Social Events

Keep it light: hobbies, travel, food, movies, sports. Ask open questions: "What do you do outside of work?", "Been on any trips lately?", "Watching anything good?".

"So, what do you get up to on weekends? — I've been getting into hiking lately. We did a great trail last weekend near Sintra."
🥂

Toasts, Celebrations & Team Events

For toasts: "I'd like to raise a glass to…", "Cheers to the team!", "Here's to a great quarter!". Celebrations: "Congratulations on the promotion!", "Well deserved!", "Happy work anniversary!".

"Before we eat, I'd like to raise a glass to Sarah — congratulations on leading our biggest project launch this year. Well deserved! Cheers!"

Work Social Do's & Don'ts

Do make an effort to attend team events — it builds goodwill.
Do include quieter colleagues — "Have you met Tom? He just joined the team."
Do keep conversations inclusive — avoid inside jokes that exclude people.
Don't drink too much at work events — stay professional.
Don't talk only about work — social events are for connecting personally.
Don't pressure colleagues who decline invitations — respect boundaries.

05 — PRACTICE

Writing Exercises

Practise your social English with the following scenarios.

Team Invitation

Write a team event invitation email

You're organising a team lunch at a new restaurant to welcome a new colleague. Write an informal email inviting your team, including the date, time, and location.

Conversation

Write a social conversation at a team lunch

You're sitting next to a colleague you don't know well at a team lunch. Write a short dialogue (6–8 exchanges) using small talk topics like hobbies, travel, or weekend plans.

Toast

Write a toast for a colleague's farewell

A colleague is leaving the company after 5 years. Write a short farewell toast (4–6 sentences) to deliver at their going-away party, highlighting their contributions and wishing them well.
