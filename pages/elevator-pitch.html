02 — THEORY

What is an Elevator Pitch?

An elevator pitch is a brief, persuasive speech that you use to introduce yourself, your product, or your idea. The name comes from the idea that you should be able to deliver it during a short elevator ride — typically 30 to 60 seconds. It's essential for networking events, job interviews, career fairs, and even casual professional encounters.

👋

The Hook

Start with something that grabs attention — a compelling question, a surprising fact, or a confident self-introduction. This is your first impression, so make it count.

"Hi, I'm Maria. Have you ever wondered why 70% of online shoppers abandon their carts before checkout?"
💼

Your Value Proposition

Explain what you do and what makes you unique. Focus on the value you bring — not just your job title. Use concrete language and avoid jargon.

"I'm a UX designer specialising in e-commerce. I help companies redesign their checkout flows to reduce cart abandonment by up to 35%."
🎯

Your Goal

Briefly state what you're looking for — a new role, a collaboration, a client, or simply to connect. Be specific but not pushy.

"I'm currently exploring opportunities at companies that are passionate about creating seamless digital experiences."
🤝

Call to Action

End with an invitation to continue the conversation. This could be exchanging contact details, scheduling a meeting, or simply asking a question.

"I'd love to hear about what your team is working on. Could we grab a coffee sometime this week?"

Key Tips for Your Elevator Pitch

Keep it under 60 seconds — practice with a timer until it feels natural.
Tailor your pitch to the audience — a recruiter wants different info than a potential client.
Avoid industry jargon — use plain, clear language that anyone can understand.
Show enthusiasm — your energy and confidence matter as much as your words.
End with a question or invitation — turn a monologue into a conversation.
Prepare 2–3 versions: a 15-second, 30-second, and 60-second version for different contexts.

05 — PRACTICE

Writing Exercises

Write your own elevator pitch using the four-step framework. Imagine you are at a networking event and someone asks "So, what do you do?"

Hook

Grab their attention

Start with your name and an engaging opening — a question, a fact, or a bold statement.

Value

Explain what you do

Describe your role and the value you bring. Focus on impact, not job titles.

Goal

State what you're looking for

Mention your current objective — a new role, a partnership, a project.

Call to Action

Invite the next step

End with something that opens the door to further conversation.
