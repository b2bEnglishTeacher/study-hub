02 — THEORY

Running Effective Meetings

Meetings are where decisions are made, ideas are shared, and projects move forward. Whether in person or on a video call, using the right language helps you contribute confidently, lead efficiently, and ensure everyone leaves with clear action items.

🎬

Opening a Meeting

Start by welcoming participants, stating the purpose, and outlining the agenda. On calls, check that everyone can hear you. Set the tone: friendly but focused.

"Good morning, everyone. Thanks for joining. Today we'll be covering three items: the Q3 results, the new marketing plan, and next steps. Shall we get started?"
🗣️

Contributing & Asking for Input

Use phrases to share your opinion, agree, disagree politely, or ask others for theirs. On calls, identify yourself before speaking if cameras are off.

"I'd like to add something here…" / "That's a good point, but have we considered…" / "Sarah, what are your thoughts on this?"
📋

Managing the Discussion

Keep things on track by redirecting off-topic conversations, managing time, and ensuring everyone has a chance to speak. Use diplomatic language to interrupt or refocus.

"That's an interesting point — could we park that for now and come back to it?" / "We're running short on time, so let's move to the next item."
✅

Closing & Action Items

Summarise key decisions, assign action items with owners and deadlines, and confirm the next meeting. Always end on time.

"To summarise: David will finalise the budget by Friday, and Maria will share the updated timeline by Monday. Our next meeting is Thursday at 10am. Thanks everyone!"

Meeting Etiquette Tips

Mute yourself when not speaking on video/phone calls.
Be punctual — joining late disrupts the flow and shows disrespect.
Use the chat function for quick comments without interrupting the speaker.
Don't multitask — give your full attention, especially on video calls.
Always send meeting minutes or a summary email after the meeting.
03 — VOCABULARY

Useful Words & Phrases

Agenda
A list of topics to be discussed
Action item
A task assigned to someone during a meeting
Minutes
A written record of what was discussed
To chair a meeting
To lead or facilitate a meeting
To park something
To set aside a topic for later discussion
To circle back
To return to a topic later
Takeaway
A key point or conclusion from a discussion
To wrap up
To bring the meeting to a close
AOB (Any Other Business)
Additional items not on the agenda
To touch on
To briefly mention or discuss a topic
Dial in
To join a meeting by phone or video
Stakeholder
Someone with interest in the meeting's outcome
