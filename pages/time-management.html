02 — THEORY

Talking About Time at Work

Time management is one of the most valued soft skills in the workplace. Being able to discuss deadlines, priorities, and workload in English — clearly and professionally — helps you manage expectations, push back when overloaded, and delegate effectively.

🎯

Setting Priorities

Use the Eisenhower Matrix to categorise tasks: urgent vs. important. Communicate priorities clearly with phrases like "This is a top priority", "This can wait", "Let's focus on the critical path first".

"I'd suggest we prioritise the client deliverable this week and push the internal report to next Monday — it's important but not urgent."
⏰

Deadlines & Timelines

Discuss deadlines precisely: "due by Friday EOD", "by close of business", "within 48 hours". If a deadline is at risk, communicate early: "I need to flag that…", "I might need an extension on…"

"The report is due by EOD Friday. If the data isn't ready by Wednesday, I'll need to flag a potential delay."
📤

Delegating Tasks

Delegation is about trusting others with work. Use clear language: "Could you take the lead on…?", "I'd like to hand this over to…", "Can I assign this to you?" Always specify the expected outcome and deadline.

"Maria, could you take the lead on the client presentation? I need the first draft by Thursday so we can review it together."
🛑

Saying No & Setting Boundaries

It's professional to push back when overloaded. Use phrases like "I don't have the bandwidth for…", "Could we reprioritise?", "I'd be happy to help, but I'd need to deprioritise X." Never just say "no" without offering an alternative.

"I'd love to take that on, but I'm at full capacity this week. Could we either push the deadline or reassign one of my current tasks?"

Productivity Tips in English

Use "EOD" (End of Day) and "COB" (Close of Business) when setting deadlines.
Time-box meetings and tasks — set a fixed duration to stay focused.
Say "Let me check my bandwidth" before committing to new work.
Use "quick win" for easy tasks with immediate value — great for team morale.
