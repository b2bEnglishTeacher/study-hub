02 — THEORY

The LEARN Framework for Complaints

Handling complaints professionally is a critical business skill. The LEARN framework provides a structured approach: Listen, Empathise, Apologise, Resolve, Notify. How you respond to a complaint often matters more than the complaint itself.

👂

Listen & Acknowledge

Let the client express their concern fully before responding. Acknowledge the issue without being defensive. Key phrases: "I understand your frustration", "Thank you for bringing this to our attention", "I can see why this is concerning".

"Thank you for bringing this to our attention, Mr. Davies. I understand how frustrating it must be to receive the wrong order after waiting two weeks."
💬

Empathise & Apologise

Show genuine understanding and take responsibility. Avoid blame-shifting. Use: "I sincerely apologise for…", "We take full responsibility for…", "This is not the standard of service we aim to provide."

"I sincerely apologise for the inconvenience this has caused. This is not the level of service we pride ourselves on, and I understand your disappointment."
🔍

Investigate & Resolve

Explain what you will do and give a clear timeline. Use: "I'm going to look into this immediately", "Here's what I can do…", "We will have this resolved by…", "As a gesture of goodwill…".

"I've escalated this to our logistics team and you will receive the correct order by Thursday. As a gesture of goodwill, we'd like to offer you a 15% discount on your next purchase."
✅

Follow Up & Prevent

Always follow up to confirm the issue was resolved. Document the complaint to prevent recurrence. Use: "I wanted to check that everything was resolved to your satisfaction", "We've implemented changes to ensure…".

"Hi Mr. Davies, I'm following up to confirm you received the correct order on Thursday. We've also updated our quality check process to prevent this from happening again."

Complaint Handling Do's & Don'ts

Do respond promptly — delays make complaints worse.
Do use the client's name — it shows respect and personalises the interaction.
Do keep a written record of all complaints and resolutions.
Don't take complaints personally or become defensive.
Don't make promises you can't keep — be realistic about timelines.
Don't use phrases like "That's not my department" — own the problem.
03 — VOCABULARY

Useful Words & Phrases

Complaint
A formal expression of dissatisfaction
To escalate
To raise an issue to a higher level of authority
To acknowledge
To recognise that a problem exists
Goodwill gesture
A voluntary act to maintain a positive relationship
To resolve
To find a solution to a problem
Compensation
Something given to make up for a loss or inconvenience
To follow up
To check on the status or outcome of something
Service recovery
The process of turning a negative experience positive
Dissatisfaction
The feeling of being unhappy with a product or service
To lodge (a complaint)
To formally submit a complaint
Recurring issue
A problem that happens repeatedly
SLA breach
Failure to meet agreed service standards

Writing Exercises

Written Response

Respond to a complaint email

Phone Script

Handle a complaint by phone

Write a script for handling this phone complaint: A customer received a damaged product and wants a replacement. Include: greeting, acknowledgement, apology, resolution, and closing.

Follow-Up

Write a follow-up email

You resolved a complaint about a billing error last week. Write a follow-up email to the client confirming the issue was fixed and asking if they need anything else.
