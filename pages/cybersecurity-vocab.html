02 — THEORY

Understanding Cyber Threats

Cybersecurity affects every employee, not just IT. Understanding the terminology helps you recognise threats, follow security protocols, and protect sensitive data.

🎣

Phishing & Social Engineering

Phishing = fraudulent emails/messages to steal data. "Spear phishing" = targeted at a specific person. "Social engineering" = manipulating people to reveal information. Key signs: urgent language, suspicious links, requests for credentials.

"The email appeared to be from the CEO requesting an urgent wire transfer — a classic spear phishing attempt."
🔒

Passwords & Authentication

"MFA/2FA" = multi/two-factor authentication. "Strong password" = 12+ characters, mixed types. "Password manager" = secure tool for storing passwords. "Single sign-on (SSO)" = one login for multiple services.

"All employees must enable 2FA on their accounts and use the company password manager for all work-related credentials."
🛡️

Data Protection & Privacy

"Encryption" = converting data to unreadable code. "GDPR" = EU data protection regulation. "Data breach" = unauthorised access to data. "PII" = Personally Identifiable Information.

"Following the data breach, we've implemented end-to-end encryption for all customer PII and updated our GDPR compliance procedures."
🔥

Malware & Incident Response

"Malware" = malicious software (viruses, ransomware, trojans). "Firewall" = a security barrier. "Incident response" = the plan for handling a breach. "Patch" = a software update fixing vulnerabilities.

"The IT team detected ransomware on three workstations. The incident response team isolated the affected machines and deployed an emergency patch."

Cybersecurity Do's & Don'ts

Do report suspicious emails immediately — even if you're not sure.
Do enable multi-factor authentication on all accounts.
Do keep software updated — patches fix security vulnerabilities.
Don't click links in unexpected emails — verify the sender first.
Don't use the same password across multiple accounts.
Don't share sensitive data over unsecured channels (public Wi-Fi, SMS).
03 — VOCABULARY

Useful Words & Phrases

Phishing
Fraudulent messages designed to steal sensitive information
Malware
Malicious software such as viruses, ransomware, and trojans
Encryption
Converting data into unreadable code for security
Firewall
A security system that monitors and controls network traffic
Data breach
Unauthorised access to confidential data
MFA
Multi-factor authentication — extra verification steps for login
Ransomware
Malware that locks data and demands payment to unlock it
Patch
A software update that fixes bugs or security vulnerabilities
VPN
Virtual Private Network — encrypts your internet connection
GDPR
EU regulation for data protection and privacy
PII
Personally Identifiable Information — data that can identify a person
Incident response
The process and plan for handling a security breach

05 — PRACTICE

Writing Exercises

Apply your cybersecurity vocabulary in the following scenarios.

Security Alert

Report a suspicious email

You received a suspicious email asking you to click a link and verify your company login credentials. Write an email to your IT department reporting the incident. Describe what happened and why you think it's a phishing attempt.

Policy Summary

Summarise a security policy

Your company has just introduced a new password policy requiring MFA, 12+ character passwords, and the use of a password manager. Write a short summary email to your team explaining the new rules and why they matter.

Incident Report

Write an incident report

Your department experienced a ransomware attack that affected several workstations. Write a brief incident report describing what happened, what actions were taken, and what steps will be implemented to prevent future incidents.
