02 — THEORY

The Language of Invoicing

Invoicing and payment communication requires precision, professionalism, and tact. Whether you're sending an invoice, negotiating payment terms, or following up on an overdue payment, the right language ensures smooth financial transactions.

📄

Issuing an Invoice

An invoice is a formal request for payment. Key elements: invoice number, date, line items, subtotal, VAT/tax, total, payment terms, bank details. Key phrases: "Please find attached invoice #…", "Payment is due within…", "The total amount due is…".

"Please find attached invoice #2026-0847 for the consulting services provided in February. The total amount due is €4,500 (excl. VAT). Payment is due within 30 days."
📅

Payment Terms

Payment terms define when and how payment should be made. Common terms: "Net 30" (pay within 30 days), "Net 60", "Due on receipt", "50% upfront, 50% on completion". "EOM" = End of Month.

"Our standard payment terms are Net 30. For new clients, we require 50% upfront and 50% upon completion of the project."
⚠️

Chasing Late Payments

Follow up professionally and firmly. Use escalating language: first reminder (friendly), second reminder (firm), final notice (formal/urgent). Key phrases: "This is a friendly reminder that…", "Payment is now X days overdue", "Please arrange payment immediately to avoid…".

"Dear Mr. Silva, this is a gentle reminder that invoice #2026-0847 was due on 15 March and is now 14 days overdue. Could you please arrange payment at your earliest convenience?"
✅

Credit Notes & Receipts

A credit note corrects or cancels an invoice (partial or full). A receipt confirms payment received. Key phrases: "We have issued credit note #… to adjust…", "We confirm receipt of your payment of €…", "Your account has been credited with…".

"Following your request, we have issued credit note #CN-0234 for €500 to reflect the revised scope. The adjusted balance is €4,000."

Invoicing Do's & Don'ts

Do include all required details on invoices — incomplete invoices cause delays.
Do send invoices promptly — the sooner you invoice, the sooner you get paid.
Do keep payment reminders professional and factual — never emotional.
Don't use threatening language in payment reminders — it damages relationships.
Don't forget to include payment instructions (bank details, reference number).
Don't ignore overdue invoices — follow up consistently and escalate when needed.
03 — VOCABULARY

Useful Words & Phrases

Invoice
A formal document requesting payment for goods or services
Payment terms
The conditions under which payment is expected
Net 30
Payment due within 30 days of the invoice date
Due date
The date by which payment must be received
Overdue
Past the payment deadline
Credit note
A document reducing the amount owed on an invoice
VAT (Value Added Tax)
A consumption tax added to the price
Line item
An individual entry on an invoice
Remittance
The act of sending payment
Outstanding balance
The amount still owed
Pro forma invoice
A preliminary invoice sent before goods/services are delivered
To settle (an account)
To pay all amounts owed

Writing Exercises

Write professional emails for the following scenarios.

Invoice Email

Send an invoice by email

Write an email attaching invoice #2026-1205 for €8,200 for web development services delivered in February. Include: reference to the project, total amount, payment terms (Net 30), and bank details reminder.

Payment Reminder

Write a payment reminder

Invoice #2026-0998 for €3,400 was due on 1 March and is now 21 days overdue. Write a second reminder (firm but professional) requesting immediate payment.

Credit Note

Issue a credit note communication

A client was overcharged by €750 on invoice #2026-1102. Write an email explaining the error, confirming the credit note has been issued, and stating the revised balance.
