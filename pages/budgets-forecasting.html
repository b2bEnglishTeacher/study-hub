01 — THEORY

Planning & Predicting in English

Budgeting and forecasting are core business activities that require precise language. Whether you're requesting budget approval, presenting a forecast, or explaining a variance, the right vocabulary gives you credibility and clarity.

💼

Budget Basics

A budget is a financial plan for a specific period. Key types: "operating budget" (day-to-day), "capital budget" (investments), "departmental budget". Key phrases: "The budget for… is…", "We've allocated €X to…", "The budget has been approved/exceeded/cut".

The marketing department's annual budget is €450K, with €180K allocated to digital campaigns and €120K to events.
📊

Forecasting & Projections

A forecast is an estimate of future performance based on data and assumptions. Key phrases: "We forecast/project/anticipate…", "Based on current trends, we expect…", "Our conservative/optimistic estimate is…".

Based on current growth trends, we forecast Q4 revenue of €5.2M — a 15% increase over Q3. This assumes no change in pricing.
⚖️

Variance & Adjustments

Variance is the difference between budgeted and actual figures. "Favourable variance" = better than planned. "Unfavourable/adverse variance" = worse than planned. Key phrases: "We are over/under budget by…", "The variance is due to…".

We are currently 8% over budget on staffing costs, primarily due to the unplanned hiring of two contractors in September.
✅

Budget Requests & Approval

Requesting budget requires justification. Key phrases: "I'd like to request approval for…", "The estimated cost is…", "The expected ROI is…", "This investment will enable us to…".

I'd like to request approval for an additional €35K for Q4 marketing. This would fund a targeted LinkedIn campaign, with an expected ROI of 3:1 based on Q2 results.

Budget Communication Do's & Don'ts

Do always justify budget requests with data and expected outcomes.
Do present both optimistic and conservative scenarios in forecasts.
Do explain variances proactively — don't wait to be asked.
Don't present a forecast as a guarantee — use hedging language ("We anticipate…").
Don't submit round numbers without supporting detail — show your working.
Don't ignore unfavourable variances — address them with a corrective action plan.
02 — VOCABULARY

Useful Words & Phrases

Budget
A financial plan outlining expected income and expenses
Forecast
A prediction of future financial performance
Allocation
The distribution of resources to specific areas
Variance
The difference between budgeted and actual figures
Favourable variance
When actual results are better than budgeted
Adverse variance
When actual results are worse than budgeted
To project
To estimate future performance based on data
Contingency
A reserve of funds for unexpected expenses
Cost overrun
Spending more than the budgeted amount
Break-even
The point where revenue equals costs
To reallocate
To move funds from one area to another
Headcount
The number of employees in a team or company

04 — PRACTICE

Writing Exercises

Write professional responses for the following scenarios.

Budget Request

Write a budget request

Request an additional €25K for your team's Q4 training budget. Justify the request with expected outcomes (improved team skills, reduced onboarding time for new hires, estimated €40K savings next year).

Forecast

Present a revenue forecast

Write a forecast paragraph for next quarter. Current Q3 revenue: €4.1M. Q4 forecast: €4.6M (optimistic) / €4.3M (conservative). Key assumptions: seasonal increase, new product launch, stable pricing.

Variance Report

Explain a budget variance

Your department is 12% over budget on travel expenses (€67K actual vs €60K budgeted). Write a brief explanation of the variance and propose corrective action.
