02 — THEORY

The Client Onboarding Journey

Client onboarding is the process of welcoming a new client and setting up the relationship for success. The language you use in these first interactions shapes trust and sets the tone for the entire partnership.

👋

Welcome & First Contact

The welcome email or call sets the tone. Be warm, professional, and clear about next steps. Key phrases: "We're delighted to welcome you…", "Thank you for choosing…", "We look forward to working together."

"Dear Ms. Tanaka, we're delighted to welcome you to [Company]. As your account manager, I'll be your main point of contact throughout the onboarding process."
🚀

Kickoff Meeting

The kickoff meeting aligns both teams on goals, scope, and timeline. Structure: introductions, project overview, roles & responsibilities, timeline, Q&A. Key phrases: "The purpose of today's meeting is to…", "Let me walk you through…".

"The purpose of today's kickoff is to align on project goals, introduce the team, and agree on key milestones for the next 90 days."
📋

Setting Expectations

Clearly communicate what the client can expect: deliverables, communication frequency, response times, escalation paths. Use phrases like "You can expect…", "We typically…", "Our standard process involves…".

"You can expect a weekly status update every Friday, and our response time for support queries is within 24 business hours."
🤝

Handover & Ongoing Support

Once onboarding is complete, transition to business-as-usual. Introduce the support team, provide documentation, and confirm the client knows who to contact.

"Now that onboarding is complete, I'd like to introduce you to Sarah, who will be your ongoing account manager. She'll schedule a monthly check-in call."

Onboarding Do's & Don'ts

Do personalise the welcome — use the client's name and reference their specific goals.
Do provide a clear timeline with milestones so the client knows what to expect.
Do confirm understanding at each stage — ask "Does this align with your expectations?"
Don't overwhelm with too much information at once — phase the onboarding.
Don't use internal jargon the client won't understand.
Don't skip the kickoff meeting — it's essential for alignment and trust-building.
03 — VOCABULARY

Useful Words & Phrases

Onboarding
The process of integrating a new client
Kickoff meeting
The initial meeting to launch a project
Point of contact
The main person responsible for communication
Milestone
A significant checkpoint in a project
Deliverable
A tangible output or result
To align (on)
To reach agreement or shared understanding
Escalation path
The process for raising unresolved issues
SLA (Service Level Agreement)
A contract defining service standards
To hand over
To transfer responsibility to another person
Stakeholder
A person with an interest in the project's outcome
Check-in
A regular meeting to review progress
To onboard
To bring a new client through the setup process
