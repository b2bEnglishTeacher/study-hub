01 — THEORY

Setting Goals That Drive Results

OKRs (Objectives and Key Results) and SMART goals are the most widely used frameworks for goal-setting in modern organisations. Understanding the vocabulary helps you set, communicate, and track goals effectively.

🎯

OKR Framework

Objective = WHAT you want to achieve (qualitative, inspiring). Key Result = HOW you measure success (quantitative, measurable). Typically set quarterly. Key phrases: "Our objective is to…", "The key results are…", "We're tracking at X% completion".

Objective: Become the #1 customer-rated platform in our category.
KR1: Achieve NPS of 60+.
KR2: Reduce support ticket resolution time to under 4 hours.
KR3: Reach 95% customer retention.
📋

SMART Goals

Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, Time-bound. Each goal should answer: What exactly? How much? Is it realistic? Does it matter? By when?

❌ "Improve marketing"
✅ "Increase organic website traffic by 25% by end of Q3 through SEO optimisation and content marketing."
📊

Tracking & Review

Regular check-ins ensure goals stay on track. Key phrases: "We're on track / at risk / behind", "The current score is…", "We need to course-correct on…". Traffic light system: Green (on track), Amber (at risk), Red (behind).

KR2 is currently amber — we're at 6.2 hours average resolution time vs our target of 4 hours. The team has identified staffing gaps as the root cause.
🔗

Alignment & Cascading

Goals should cascade: company → department → team → individual. "Alignment" ensures everyone works toward the same outcomes. Key phrases: "This aligns with our company objective of…", "Your team goal supports…".

Your team's objective to reduce churn by 10% directly supports the company-level objective of achieving €5M ARR by year-end.

Goal-Setting Do's & Don'ts

Do make objectives inspiring and key results measurable.
Do review OKRs regularly — monthly check-ins at minimum.
Do cascade goals so individual work connects to company strategy.
Don't set too many OKRs — 3-5 objectives with 3-4 key results each is ideal.
Don't confuse tasks with key results — KRs measure outcomes, not activities.
Don't punish teams for missing stretch goals — OKRs encourage ambition.
02 — VOCABULARY

Useful Words & Phrases

Objective
A qualitative, inspiring goal you want to achieve
Key result
A measurable outcome that shows progress toward an objective
SMART goal
A goal that is Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, Time-bound
KPI
Key Performance Indicator — a metric used to evaluate success
To cascade
To pass goals down through levels of an organisation
Alignment
Ensuring all teams work toward the same strategic outcomes
Milestone
A significant checkpoint or achievement along the way
Stretch goal
An ambitious target that pushes beyond what seems easily achievable
Quarterly review
A check-in every three months to assess goal progress
To track
To monitor and measure progress over time
Accountability
Being responsible for delivering on commitments
North star metric
The single most important metric that reflects core value delivery
