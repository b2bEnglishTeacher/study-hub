01 — THEORY

The Language of Data

Being able to describe data clearly and accurately is one of the most valuable skills in business. Whether you're presenting quarterly results or writing a report, the right vocabulary makes your message powerful and credible.

📈

Describing Trends

Use precise verbs to describe movement: "increase/rise/grow" (up), "decrease/decline/drop/fall" (down), "remain steady/plateau/stabilise" (flat), "fluctuate" (up and down). Add adverbs for degree: "sharply", "gradually", "slightly", "significantly".

Sales increased sharply in Q1, rising by 28%, before plateauing in Q2 at around €2.1M.
📊

Describing Charts & Graphs

Reference visuals professionally: "As shown in Figure 1…", "The bar chart illustrates…", "The pie chart breaks down…", "According to the data…". Describe what the data shows, then interpret it.

As shown in Figure 3, the line graph illustrates a steady upward trend in monthly active users, from 12K in January to 19K in June.
🎯

KPIs & Metrics

Key Performance Indicators (KPIs) are measurable values that track progress. Common KPIs: "conversion rate", "customer acquisition cost (CAC)", "customer lifetime value (CLV)", "NPS (Net Promoter Score)", "MRR". Use: "Our target for… is…", "We're tracking at…".

Our NPS improved from 42 to 58 this quarter, exceeding our target of 50. Customer acquisition cost dropped to €45, down 12% from Q2.
🔢

Percentages, Ratios & Comparisons

Use precise language: "X accounts for 35% of total revenue", "The ratio of X to Y is 3:1", "compared to/with", "relative to", "as opposed to". For changes: "by 10%" (the amount), "to 10%" (the new level), "from 10% to 15%".

Online sales account for 62% of total revenue, compared to just 38% from retail — a complete reversal from five years ago.

Data Presentation Do's & Don'ts

Do lead with the insight, not the data — tell the audience what the numbers mean.
Do use consistent units and timeframes when comparing data.
Do distinguish between correlation and causation when interpreting trends.
Don't overload slides with numbers — highlight 2-3 key takeaways per chart.
Don't use vague language ("sales went up a bit") — be specific ("sales rose by 8%").
Don't present data without context — always compare to targets, benchmarks, or prior periods.
02 — VOCABULARY

Useful Words & Phrases

KPI
Key Performance Indicator; a measurable value tracking progress
Trend
The general direction in which something is developing
To plateau
To reach a level and stay there without further change
To fluctuate
To rise and fall irregularly
Year-on-year (YoY)
Comparing data to the same period last year
Quarter-on-quarter (QoQ)
Comparing data to the previous quarter
Benchmark
A standard against which performance is measured
To peak
To reach the highest point
To account for
To represent a specific proportion of a total
Correlation
A relationship between two variables
Metric
A quantifiable measure used to track performance
Forecast
A prediction of future trends based on data
