01 — THEORY

Professional Phone Skills

Despite the rise of email and messaging, phone calls remain essential in business. Answering professionally, transferring calls smoothly, and leaving clear voicemails are skills that set you apart.

📞

Answering & Identifying

Answer with your name and company: "Good morning, [Company], [Name] speaking. How can I help you?" Ask who's calling: "May I ask who's calling?", "Could I have your name, please?". Clarify purpose: "How can I help you today?".

"Good morning, Apex Solutions, Maria speaking. How can I help you? — Hi Maria, it's David Chen from TechVentures. I'm calling about the proposal we discussed last week."
🔀

Transferring & Holding

"I'll put you through to…", "Let me transfer you to our [department]", "Would you mind holding for a moment?", "I'm afraid [Name] is in a meeting. Can I take a message?".

"Let me transfer you to our accounts department — they'll be able to help with your invoice query. Would you mind holding for just a moment?"
📝

Taking & Leaving Messages

Taking: "Can I take a message?", "I'll make sure [Name] gets your message". Leaving voicemail: state your name, company, reason for calling, callback number, and best time. Keep it under 30 seconds.

"Hi, this is Maria Silva from Apex Solutions. I'm calling regarding the partnership proposal we sent on Monday. Could you call me back at 555-0142? I'm available until 5pm today. Thank you."
👋

Closing & Follow-up

Summarise action points before hanging up. Key phrases: "So just to confirm…", "I'll send you a follow-up email", "Thank you for your time", "Have a great day".

"So just to confirm — I'll send the revised proposal by Friday, and we'll schedule a follow-up call for next Tuesday. Thanks for your time, David. Have a great day!"

Phone Call Do's & Don'ts

Do smile when you speak — it genuinely changes your tone of voice.
Do speak clearly and at a moderate pace — especially on international calls.
Do confirm key details by repeating them back ("So that's Friday at 3pm?").
Don't put someone on hold without asking first.
Don't eat, drink, or multitask audibly during calls.
Don't leave voicemails longer than 30 seconds — be concise.
02 — VOCABULARY

Useful Words & Phrases

To put through
To connect a caller to another person or department
To hold (the line)
To wait on the phone without hanging up
Voicemail
A recorded message left when someone can't answer
Extension
An internal phone number within a company
To return a call
To call someone back after they called you
Callback
A return phone call, often requested or scheduled
To dial
To enter a phone number to make a call
Conference call
A phone call with three or more participants
To patch someone in
To add someone to an ongoing phone call
Caller ID
A feature that displays the caller's phone number or name
To hang up
To end a phone call by disconnecting
Reception / Switchboard
The front desk or system that routes incoming calls

04 — PRACTICE

Writing Exercises

Practise your phone skills by writing scripts for the following scenarios.

Answer a Call

Answer and transfer a call

Write a short phone conversation script where you answer a call at your company, identify the caller, and transfer them to the correct department.

Leave a Voicemail

Leave a professional voicemail

You called a client but they didn't answer. Leave a clear, concise voicemail (under 30 seconds) stating your name, company, reason for calling, and callback number.

Take a Message

Take a message for a colleague

Your colleague is in a meeting. Write a phone conversation script where you politely explain they're unavailable and take a detailed message from the caller.
