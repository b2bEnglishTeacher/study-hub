02 — THEORY

The Art of Proofreading

Proofreading is the final check before publishing or sending — it catches errors that undermine your credibility. A single typo in a client email or a grammar mistake in a report can change how people perceive your professionalism. Mastering this skill is essential for anyone who writes in English at work.

🔤

Spelling & Commonly Confused Words

English has many tricky pairs: affect/effect, their/there/they're, its/it's, principal/principle, compliment/complement. Also watch for British vs American spelling (organisation/organization, colour/color).

The new policy will affect (not effect) all departments. Its (not it's) implementation begins Monday.
📐

Grammar & Sentence Structure

Common errors: subject-verb agreement ("The team are" vs "The team is"), dangling modifiers, run-on sentences, and incorrect tense usage. Read sentences aloud to catch awkward phrasing.

❌ Having reviewed the data, the report was submitted.
✅ Having reviewed the data, the team submitted the report.
✏️

Punctuation & Formatting

Master the comma splice, semicolons, apostrophes, and colons. Common errors: "it's" vs "its", missing Oxford comma, overusing exclamation marks. Formatting matters too: consistent headings, bullet styles, and spacing.

The project includes three phases: discovery, development, and deployment. (Note the Oxford comma and colon usage.)
🎯

Clarity, Conciseness & Tone

Cut unnecessary words. Replace passive voice where it weakens the message. Check that your tone matches the audience. "At this point in time" → "now", "in order to" → "to", "utilise" → "use".

❌ We would like to take this opportunity to inform you that…
✅ We're pleased to announce that…

Proofreading Checklist

Do read your text aloud — your ear catches errors your eye misses.
Do proofread backwards (sentence by sentence) to focus on individual errors.
Do use spell-check tools, but never rely on them alone — they miss context errors.
Don't proofread immediately after writing — take a break first.
Don't only check for typos — also review for clarity, tone, and consistency.
Don't assume long words make you sound smarter — simple language is more professional.
03 — VOCABULARY

Useful Words & Phrases

Typo
A small error in typed or printed text
To proofread
To read and correct errors in a text
Clarity
The quality of being clear and easy to understand
Concise
Expressing much in few words; brief and clear
Tone
The attitude or feeling conveyed by the writing
Run-on sentence
Two or more sentences incorrectly joined
Subject-verb agreement
Matching singular/plural subjects with verbs
Oxford comma
A comma before "and" in a list of three or more
Passive voice
A sentence where the subject receives the action
Dangling modifier
A phrase that doesn't clearly relate to the word it modifies
Consistency
Using the same style, format, or terminology throughout
To revise
To re-examine and make corrections to a text

Rewrite for Clarity

Simplify this text

Rewrite the following in clearer, more concise English: "We would like to take this opportunity to inform you that, in accordance with the aforementioned policy changes, the utilisation of the company parking facilities will be subject to a new set of regulations effective as of the first day of the upcoming month."

Tone Check

Adjust the tone
