02 — THEORY

Understanding SWOT Analysis

A SWOT analysis is a strategic planning tool used to evaluate internal factors (Strengths and Weaknesses) and external factors (Opportunities and Threats). It's used in business planning, project evaluation, career development, and competitive analysis. Being able to discuss SWOT in English is essential for any professional setting.

S

Strengths (Internal, Positive)

What does the company/person do well? What advantages do they have? Strengths are internal resources, skills, or assets that give a competitive edge.

"Our key strengths include a highly skilled workforce, strong brand recognition, and proprietary technology that competitors lack."
W

Weaknesses (Internal, Negative)

What could be improved? What are the limitations? Weaknesses are internal factors that put the organisation at a disadvantage.

"A notable weakness is our limited online presence compared to competitors, and our reliance on a single revenue stream."
O

Opportunities (External, Positive)

What external trends or changes could benefit the company? Opportunities are favourable conditions in the market, industry, or environment.

"The growing demand for sustainable products represents a significant opportunity for us to expand into eco-friendly product lines."
T

Threats (External, Negative)

What external factors could cause problems? Threats are challenges from competitors, regulations, market changes, or economic conditions.

"New regulations on data privacy pose a threat to our current business model, requiring significant investment in compliance."

Tips for Presenting a SWOT

Be specific — avoid vague statements like "good team". Say what makes them good.
Prioritise — list the most important factors first, not everything you can think of.
Link SWOT to action: strengths to leverage, weaknesses to address, opportunities to pursue, threats to mitigate.
Remember: S and W are internal (you control them), O and T are external (you respond to them).
03 — VOCABULARY

Useful Words & Phrases

Competitive advantage
Something that puts you ahead of competitors
Market share
Percentage of total sales in an industry
To leverage
To use a strength to your advantage
To mitigate
To reduce the impact of a risk or threat
Gap in the market
An unmet need or untapped opportunity
Revenue stream
A source of income for a business
Regulatory compliance
Following laws and regulations
Core competency
A key ability that defines a business
Market disruption
A significant change that transforms an industry
Scalability
Ability to grow without losing efficiency
To capitalise on
To take advantage of a favourable situation
Vulnerability
A weakness that can be exploited

05 — PRACTICE

Writing Exercises

Conduct a SWOT analysis in English. Choose a company you know well (or use your own) and complete each quadrant.

Strengths

What are the key strengths?

List 3 internal strengths with specific details.

Weaknesses

What are the key weaknesses?

List 3 internal weaknesses that need improvement.

Opportunities

What external opportunities exist?

List 3 external opportunities the company could pursue.

Threats

What external threats are there?

List 3 external threats that could impact the business.
