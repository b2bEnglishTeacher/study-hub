01 — THEORY

The Language of Great Leaders

Leadership is, above all, communication. The words you choose shape culture, inspire action, and build trust. Great leaders use language intentionally — to motivate, to align, and to empower.

🎯

Communicating Vision

A vision statement paints a picture of the future. Use present tense and aspirational language: "Our vision is to…", "We are building a company that…", "By 2030, we will…". Avoid vague buzzwords.

"Our vision is to become the most trusted partner for SMEs in Europe, helping 10,000 businesses digitise their operations by 2028."
💪

Motivating & Inspiring

Use inclusive language ("we", "our team"), recognise achievements publicly, and connect daily work to the bigger purpose. Key phrases: "I believe in this team's ability to…", "What you've achieved is remarkable", "This work matters because…".

"What you've achieved this quarter is remarkable — 40% growth while maintaining our quality standards. This is what sets our team apart."
🤝

Empowerment & Delegation

Empower by giving ownership, not just tasks. Use: "I trust your judgement on this", "You have full ownership of…", "I'd like you to lead this initiative". Avoid micromanaging language.

"I'd like you to lead the client onboarding redesign. You have full ownership — just keep me updated at our weekly sync."
📋

Accountability & Feedback

Hold people accountable with clarity, not blame. Use: "Let's discuss what happened and how we can improve", "What support do you need?", "The expectation going forward is…".

"The deadline was missed, and I want to understand what happened so we can prevent it. What support do you need to get back on track?"

Leadership Communication Do's & Don'ts

Do use "we" more than "I" — leadership is collective.
Do be specific with praise — "Great job on the client presentation" beats "Good work".
Do communicate the "why" behind decisions — people follow purpose, not orders.
Don't use fear or pressure as motivation — it's unsustainable.
Don't make promises you can't keep — trust is built on consistency.
Don't avoid difficult conversations — address issues early and directly.
02 — VOCABULARY

Useful Words & Phrases

Vision
A clear picture of the future a leader wants to create
Mission
The purpose and core objective of an organisation
Empowerment
Giving people authority and confidence to act
Accountability
Taking responsibility for outcomes and commitments
Delegation
Assigning tasks and authority to others
Alignment
Ensuring everyone works toward the same goals
Stakeholder
A person with interest or concern in an organisation
To inspire
To fill someone with motivation and enthusiasm
To mentor
To guide and support someone's professional growth
Transparency
Open and honest communication with no hidden agendas
Buy-in
Agreement and support from others for a plan or idea
Culture
The shared values, behaviours, and norms of a team

04 — PRACTICE

Writing Exercises

Practice leadership communication with the following scenarios.

Vision Statement

Craft a vision statement

You are the CEO of a growing tech startup. Write a compelling vision statement (3–5 sentences) that inspires your team and clearly communicates where the company is headed.

Team Motivation

Write a motivational message

Your team has just completed a challenging project under a tight deadline. Write a motivational message (4–6 sentences) recognising their effort and connecting it to the bigger picture.

Difficult Conversation

Address a performance issue

A team member has been consistently missing deadlines. Write a message (4–6 sentences) addressing the issue with clarity and empathy, focusing on improvement rather than blame.
