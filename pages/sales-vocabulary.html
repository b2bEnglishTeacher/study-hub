02 — THEORY

The Sales Process in English

Understanding sales vocabulary is critical for anyone working in business development, account management, or client-facing roles. The sales process follows a structured pipeline from lead generation to closing.

🔎

Prospecting & Lead Generation

The first stage is finding potential customers. Key terms: "cold call" (contacting someone without prior relationship), "warm lead" (someone who has shown interest), "inbound lead" (comes to you), "outbound lead" (you reach out).

"Our SDR team generated 45 qualified leads through outbound prospecting this quarter."
📊

Pipeline & Qualification

The pipeline is a visual representation of where each deal stands. Qualification means determining if a prospect is a good fit. Common frameworks: BANT (Budget, Authority, Need, Timeline).

"We have 12 deals in the pipeline worth a total of €340K. Three are in the qualification stage."
🤝

Negotiation & Closing

Negotiation involves discussing terms, pricing, and conditions. Closing is securing the commitment. Key phrases: "to close a deal", "to sign off on", "to reach an agreement", "win rate".

"After three rounds of negotiation, we closed the deal at €85K — a 15% discount from list price."
📈

Conversion & Metrics

Conversion is when a lead becomes a paying customer. Key metrics: "conversion rate", "average deal size", "sales cycle length", "churn rate", "MRR" (Monthly Recurring Revenue).

"Our conversion rate improved from 18% to 24% after implementing the new demo process."

Sales Communication Do's & Don'ts

Do listen more than you talk — understand the client's needs before pitching.
Do use data and case studies to support your value proposition.
Do follow up consistently — most deals require 5+ touchpoints.
Don't use aggressive or pushy language — build trust instead.
Don't overpromise on deliverables or timelines to close a deal.
Don't ignore objections — address them directly and honestly.
03 — VOCABULARY

Useful Words & Phrases

Lead
A potential customer who has shown interest
Prospect
A qualified potential customer
Pipeline
A visual overview of deals at each stage
To close (a deal)
To finalise a sale or agreement
Conversion rate
The percentage of leads that become customers
Cold call
Contacting someone without prior relationship
Churn rate
The rate at which customers stop doing business
Upsell
Selling a higher-value product to an existing customer
Quota
A sales target set for a specific period
Commission
Earnings based on sales performance
ROI (Return on Investment)
Financial benefit compared to cost
Touchpoint
Any interaction between a company and a customer

05 — PRACTICE

Writing Exercises

Write professional responses for the following scenarios.

Pipeline Review

Describe a sales pipeline

You are presenting your team's Q3 pipeline to the sales director. Write a short summary covering: total deals, total value, deals at each stage, and expected close dates.

Cold Email

Write a prospecting email

Write a cold outreach email to a potential client introducing your company's project management software. Keep it short, value-focused, and include a clear call to action.

Objection Handling

Handle a price objection

A prospect says: 'Your solution is 30% more expensive than the competitor.' Write a professional response that acknowledges the concern and reframes the value.
